Core thesis
  • Intelligence scales faster than responsibility. Without explicit authority, decisions drift and accountability dissolves.
  • Execution is not value if it cannot be governed. Autonomous actions require mandates, constraints, and escalation paths.
  • Control compounds. The authority layer survives model cycles, vendor churn, and organizational reshuffles.
WHAT IT IS

Decision authority infrastructure that binds mandates, permissions, and accountability to execution.

WHY IT MATTERS

Authority by design

mandates

Define who can decide, under what conditions, and how decisions are audited and revised.

  • Role-based mandates and bounded autonomy
  • Escalation paths and human-in-the-loop triggers

Execution continuity

enforcement

Carry decisions through execution across tools, teams, and vendors, even as conditions change.

  • Policy enforcement across systems
  • Outcome accountability and drift detection

Governance telemetry

audit

Make authority observable: what was decided, by whom, with what evidence, and what changed.

  • Decision logs, approvals, and overrides
  • Compliance-ready audit trails

How it fits a control stack

architecture
